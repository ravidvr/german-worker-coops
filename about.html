I built this map because I was curious: where are all the worker-owned businesses in Germany? Turns out, nobody had put them on a map before.

How the data was collected

Three public sources → merged → deduplicated → geocoded → published.

I scraped worker-coops.de (44 entries, curated by worker-coop advocates), Wikipedia's list of worker cooperatives (German entries), and the GitHub tech-coops directory. All three were merged, deduplicated by name, and classified by governance model (worker majority, governance-based, statute-based, or in founding).

Each business was then geocoded to its street address using OpenStreetMap's Nominatim API. For addresses that weren't obvious, I visited their websites directly and extracted the address from their Impressum pages.

The result: 62 verified worker-owned businesses across 15 sectors — from IT co-ops in Berlin to carpentry workshops in Bavaria, food co-ops in Leipzig to roofing co-ops in Bremen.

Every entry is attributed to its source. The database is CC BY-SA 4.0. New entries can be submitted via the map's "Add a business" form — they're reviewed and added within hours.
